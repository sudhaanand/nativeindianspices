Black Pepper (Piper nigrum)

Black Pepper is native to the southernmost Malabar region of India and is a well-known spice used in cuisines worldwide.

Pepper is derived from the seeds or drupes of the plant. The drupes are harvested when they are mature and start turning red, they are then sun or oven dried to remove moisture and turn black due to oxidation that produces the black beads we are familiar with.

Black pepper has excellent medicinal properties and is often used to cure common colds and digestive ailments. In Indian cooking it is used in several spice blends and as a seasoning.
