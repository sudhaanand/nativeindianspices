Curry Leaves (Murraya koenigii)

Curry Leaves are the leaves of a small tree that are aromatic and grows well in tropical climates. Even though they are called Curry leaves, they are not used in curries, but rather used as a seasoning element or ground with other ingredients to make spice powders and dips.

The leaves have a dark green tinge and shine to their surface and are said to have medicinal properties. They are extensively used in India, Sri Lanka, and other Asian countries.
