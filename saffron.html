Saffron (Crocus sativus)

Saffron is one of the most expensive spices in the world and is used as a condiment and coloring agent in cooking. Saffron threads are the dried stigmas of the crocus sativus flower and in India they are grown exclusively in the northern most state of Kashmir. Considered to be one of the best quality saffron, they contain large amounts of picro-crocin(color) and safranal(flavor).

Saffron also known as Kesar in India has a subtle taste and is used in sweet and savory dishes. The delicate threads release their color and flavor and result in a rich dish worthy of royalty.

They are also used for medicinal purposes as they are considered good for the skin and also have brain-simulating and aphrodisiac properties.
