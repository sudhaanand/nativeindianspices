Spices, the essence of Indian food

People often associate the word spice with the level of heat in a dish, but in Indian cooking, spices are responsible for the layers of flavor both subtle and intense. India has been one of the centers of spice trade for centuries, as it is home to several spices that are native to the sub-continent.

India being such a vast country, the usage of spices differs widely by the cooking style of the region and the local people. Indian cooking also follows the adage that food is medicine and several of the spices used in cooking have extraordinary medicinal properties. Spices are combined and used differently in various recipes, a subtle shift in cooking technique and even the order they are used leads to entirely different flavors.

The food an Indian kitchen produces is only as good as the spices it houses and the treasure of an Indian kitchen is the spice box. Spice boxes are unique containers with several cubby holes to store different spices and their combinations which by itself is a treat to the eyes and makes cooking a joy.

In this resource I have tried to explain a little about some of the major spices that are native to India.
