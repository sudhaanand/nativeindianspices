Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum)

Green Cardamom is native to the southernmost forest and hilly regions of India and favor the wet and shady tropical climate. They are often grown in combination with pepper vines that climb of trees and provide the needed shade.

The actual spice is in the form of dried pods that are home to small dark/black seeds that have a unique fragrance and impart a pungent but sweet flavor with mild citrus notes. Sometimes the pods are blanched to improve shelf life and become white in the process.

Green cardamom is an excellent digestive and breath freshener and is used to season desserts and rice dishes. They are also used in spice mixes to flavor various dishes.
